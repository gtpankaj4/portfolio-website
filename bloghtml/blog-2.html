Nepal's Condition Right Now - Should We Be Worried?

Published on April 11th, 2024 by Pankaj Bhatta

Nepal - a country of joy, a country of brave Gurkhas, a country of biodiversity, a country of cultural heritage sites, a country of dark past, and not to forget a country of mine - has been suffering a really bad phase for years. Many youths have started hating Nepal intentionally or unintentionally, but they have. Do you know, why?

When I asked you why, you must have a word buzzing in your mind if you are Nepali. Was it "POLITICS"? If yes, Congratulations and if not, maybe it was something that would directly or indirectly link to politics. Nevertheless, Is it politics that ruined our beautiful, awesome, gorgeous - well, I'm lacking adjectives to explain my country - nation? Let's find out!

Before this goes on, I don't have any statistical evidence for these points on this blog. This is my opinion and truly based on my own opinions. If you don't think I'm right, I am so sorry for that. Let's get back to our topic. What do you guys think even if the youths become mayors or some sort of political leaders, can they do their work without any hindrances? No! Those who just lost their power start fake allegations against the one who is in power. And, you know what happens? The guy who was lately in power makes the youth a clown towards civilians. Next, A talented leader makes a project to develop a place. For a project to pass, Corruption-1.For budget, Corruption-2. For companies to invest, Corruption-3. For........ Corruption 4...5...6......n!

We, civilians, always become dull during elections. But, active while poking or roasting those political leaders after election for 5 years. After 5 years, that political leader says unbelievable things will happen if he comes to power, people vote for them, and Hallelujah! He wins! He does nothing and again citizens start poking, roasting them. This cycle continues! We can't totally blame these politicians in this case. We are also responsible for this and we must accept this. It isn't that your constituency chose a good leader, it must be of all constituencies nationwide. Then, we can expect a proper development of our country.

Do you think youths will stay in this country now? Will they really do? I don't think they will. If you go through your internet and look at the statistics for people leaving the country, you'll be flabbergasted. Statistics don't lie and neither does the scenario at TIA. Youths not getting employment, Students not getting quality education, poor health care facilities, unfair advantage for power, corruption, and so on are the basic reasons for brain drain.

I don't think I have more to say on this topic. If later I want to add something, I'll just update the blog. For that, please keep on checking timely.

Thankyou so much !!
